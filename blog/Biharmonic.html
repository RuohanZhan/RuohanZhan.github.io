Biharmonic Interpolation

minimium curvature interpolation, November 5, 2016
Notes for this page.

Consider PDE:

(1-T)∇4f(x,y)-(T) ∇2f(x,y)=0

with the following boundary condition

  • (1-T)∂2f/∂n2+(T)∂f/∂n=0;

  • ∂∇2f/∂n=0 on the edge;

  • ∂2f/(∂x∂y)=0 at the corner.

    • If T=0

    Then it's a pure biharmonic function, which cares more on the "bending" energy.

    If T=1

    Then we solve Laplacian equation, then each point is either "saddle point" or flat, which cares more on the "streching" energy. It's known as "minimum energy surface" which has a good example: "shaping a film of soap".

    If T\in[0,1]

    Then both "bending" and "streching" energy will be considered.

    For instance, if you chop off the peak of a mountain, then in T=0 case, possibily we could restore a mountain shape; in T=1 case, it would look flattish.

    More reference:
    Sandwell D T. Biharmonic spline interpolation of GEOS-3 and SEASAT altimeter data[J]. Geophysical research letters, 1987, 14(2): 139-142.
