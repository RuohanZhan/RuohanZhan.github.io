Multi-scale Nonlocal Wavelet Transformation

self-recursive prior, November 5, 2016
Notes for paper:
Yuhui Quan, Hui Ji, Zuowei Shen, Data-driven multi-scale non-local wavelet frame construction and image recovery, Journal of Scientific Computing, 63(2) (2015), 307-329.
Motivation:

Integrate the following three priors:

  • sparse nature of local intensity variations of images;

  • self-recursive prior of local image structure in the spatial domain;

  • self-recursive prior of local image structure in the different scales.

    • BUild multi-scale nonlocal wavelet frames:

    Suppose data is $f$. Then the multi-scale nonlocal wavelet frames are $D=1/√2[W;J(f)W]$. It can be proved that $D$ is a frame system, and $D$ is a tight frame as long as $J(f)$ is a tight frame.

    Modified J(f) is a grouping(wavelet coefficients) matrix, and assigns different weights within each group using a collection of similarity-based low-pass filters. The regularization term is a l1-penalty on Wf and J(f)Wf.

    Comments

  • Regularizing $l_1$ penalty on J(f)Wf is to punish mean value of wavelet coefficients in the same group.

  • This paper uses a one-dimensional wavelet transformation, and two dimensional version may capture more information.

  • A data-driven learned dictionary could be used to replace W.
