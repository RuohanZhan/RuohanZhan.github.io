CT Image Reconstruction by Spatial-Radon Domain Data-Driven Tight Frame Regularization [PDF] [Code]

Ruohan Zhan, Bin Dong.
SIAM Journal on Imaging Sciences, 9(3), 1063-1083,2016.

In this project, we propose a spatial-Radon domain CT image reconstruction model based on data-driven tight frames (SRD-DDTF). The proposed SRD-DDTF model combines the idea of joint image and Radon domain inpainting model and that of the data-driven tight frames for image denoising. It is different from existing models in that both CT image and ts corresponding high quality projection image are reconstructed simultaneously using sparsity priors by tight frames that are adaptively learned from the data to provide optimal sparse approximations. An alternative minimization algorithm is designed to solve the proposed model which is nonsmooth and nonconvex. Convergence analysis of the algorithm is provided. Numerical experiments showed that the SRD-DDTF model is superior to the previous model especially in recovering some subtle structures in the images.

De-reflection via Two Wavelet Systems Code

Ruohan Zhan, mentored by Prof. Zuowei Shen and Prof. Hui Ji.

Layer separation for images taken through window is an under-determined problem, which is hard to solve especially in the ambiguity in low frequency layers. Ghosting phenomenon breaks symmetry in two layers, but could cause displacement for replicate patterns and edges in the similar direction with ghosting kernel. In this project, we propose a two-wavelet-system approach to achieve both low-frequency part separation and edge preservation. The first wavelet system is to decompose image into low-frequency part and high-frequency part, and the second wavelet system is to facilitate low-frequency separation. We then put up with a two-stage approximation algorithm based on variable splitting to solve our model. Further analysis on the algorithm gives insights to the ill-posed layer separation problem that high-pass wavelet enhancement could largely contribute to low-frequency regularization due to the redundancy of wavelet system. Numerical experiments on real world data show that our approach could achieve comparable de-reflection results with previous methods, and notably better preserve edges in IT . Besides, our approach is more computational efficient due to the fast linear wavelet transformation.

Machine Learning in Echocardiography

Baichuan Yuan, Sathya R. Chitturi, Geoffrey Iyer, Nuoyu Li, Xiaochuan Xu, Ruohan Zhan, Rafael Llerena, Jesse T. Yen, Andrea L. Bertozzi.
SPIE Medical Imaging 2017

Our work focuses on applying machine learning methods to facilitating echocardiographic diagnosis. Firstly, Non-negative Matrix Factorization(NMF) is used to extract low dimensional representation of video frames. Biographically meaningful end-members are produced and used to select end-systolic and end-diastolic frames of cardiac cycle. Cardiac period is estimated based on the analysis of NMF coefficients, followed by a video decomposition to capture different features of heart motion. Numerical results show that our method is more accurate and more operable than previous manifold learning methods.

Applications of Split Bregman and its Linearised Version to Neural Network TrainingSplit [Notes] [Code]

Ruohan Zhan, discussed with Prof. Stanley Osher, Prof. Yuan Yao and Prof. Wotao Yin.

With the advent of big data era, neural network, as a powerful data learning tool, has drawn increasing attention, leading to the upsurge of researches on how to tune it effectively and efficiently. Traditional methods as back propagation suffer from saturation effects(known as vanishing gradients), especially dealing with deep neural networks. In this project, we study an up-to-date tuning method, that is, to apply split bregman iteration to solving an optimisation model with equality constraints. Numerical results show its tuning efficiency as well as the ability of avoiding saturation. Our work furthermore extends it to linearized split bregman iteration, which has shown the potential of being more computationally scalable due to the drop of inverse calculation by preliminary results.
